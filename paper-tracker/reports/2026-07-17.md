- `analysis_en`：The main contribution of MIDAS Hand is an open, low-cost, human-scale dexterous hand platform with integrated tactile sensing, together with a released software stack for control, tactile APIs, simulation, retargeting, and teleoperation. It addresses the practical bottlenecks that often slow dexterous-hand research: expensive hardware, difficult maintenance, poor tactile integration, and high integration overhead across platforms. The method combines 16 total DoF, 13 active DoF, 283 three-axis tactile taxels, direct-drive actuation, and modular 3D-printed construction into one reproducible system. The public evaluation includes workspace and grasp-taxonomy analysis, payload and reliability tests, backdrivability measurements, and teleoperation demonstrations with tactile sensing. The reported result is a balanced research platform at roughly 700 g and under USD 3,000 that is positioned for tactile dexterous manipulation and human-to-robot data collection. In summary, this is a strong infrastructure contribution for the field because it lowers the barrier to experimentation and improves reproducibility; its limitation is that the public release still leaves some questions open about large-scale task benchmarking, long-term robustness, and transfer across tasks. citeturn0search0
