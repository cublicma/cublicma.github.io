## 1. Closing the Reality Gap: Zero-Shot Sim-to-Real Deployment for Dexterous Force-Based Grasping and Manipulation

该工作主要提出一种面向五指灵巧手的零样本仿真到真实部署流程，重点放在力驱动的抓取与操作。它解决的是灵巧手控制中仿真训练容易、真实部署难，以及力反馈在接触丰富场景下难以直接迁移的问题。方法上，公开信息表明作者围绕力状态、动作转换和策略迁移构建了端到端方案，但本轮可见材料尚未完整展开模型细节与训练管线。实验部分显示该方法在真实灵巧手平台上进行了验证，并强调无需额外真实场景微调即可落地；从题目和摘要可判断其核心结果是缩小了仿真与真实之间的现实差距。总体来看，这是一项把灵巧手力控部署问题直接摆到台前的工作，适合追踪其后续代码和完整实验披露；限制在于当前公开信息对具体硬件、基线、失败案例和定量提升的说明仍不充分。

The main contribution is a zero-shot sim-to-real deployment pipeline for five-finger dexterous hands, centered on force-based grasping and manipulation. It addresses a common dexterous-control problem: policies can often be trained in simulation, but transferring force-sensitive behavior to real hands remains difficult because contact dynamics are hard to match and real-world adaptation is expensive. Methodologically, the public information suggests an end-to-end setup built around force-state reasoning, action transfer, and policy deployment, although the current materials do not fully expose the model internals or training recipe. The experiments are reported on a real dexterous-hand platform and emphasize deployment without additional real-world fine-tuning, which indicates that the method is aimed at narrowing the sim-to-real gap in practice. Overall, this is a focused contribution on deployable dexterous force control, and it is worth tracking for code release and fuller experimental disclosure; the limitation is that the available public material still leaves the hardware, baselines, failure cases, and quantitative gains under-specified.

## 2. Mask2Real-WM: Segmentation Masks as a Sim-to-Real Bridge for Controllable Dexterous World Models

该工作主要提出 `Mask2Real-WM`，把 23 DoF 机器人手的精细控制与世界模型结合起来，目标是让高自由度灵巧手在复杂接触与细粒度动作下更稳定地执行任务。它解决的是高维手部控制中状态空间大、接触变化快、规划与控制难以兼顾的问题。方法上，公开信息显示作者使用了基于分割掩码的 sim-to-real 桥接思路，并配套项目页与代码说明来支持后续复现，但本轮尚未完整读取到论文正文，因此具体结构、损失函数和数据管线仍需进一步确认。实验与结果方面，可确认其面向真实机器人手控制而非泛化的抓取演示，且公开摘要给出一套利用自监督学习、基于视觉引导的世界模型与策略控制的方案；不过当前可见材料不足以细化具体定量指标。总体来看，这是一项与灵巧手核心控制直接相关的工程与方法结合型工作，潜在价值在于提高 23 DoF 手的控制精度和稳定性；限制是公开摘要层面的信息仍不够完整，尚不能充分评估其泛化能力和真实环境鲁棒性。

The main contribution is `Mask2Real-WM`, a dexterous world model built around segmentation masks as a sim-to-real bridge for controllable dexterous world models. It tackles a difficult dexterous-manipulation problem: high-dimensional hand control makes it hard to jointly model contact-rich dynamics, planning, and precise execution. Methodologically, the public information indicates a segmentation-mask-based bridge plus a world-model/control stack, along with a project page and code trail that should support reproducibility, but the current materials do not yet expose the full architecture, losses, or data pipeline. On the experimental side, the item is clearly hand-centric and aimed at real robot-hand control rather than generic grasp demos, and the project materials suggest visualizations and system-level implementation; however, the public evidence available in this run does not provide enough detail to state specific quantitative metrics. Overall, it is a relevant method-and-system contribution for dexterous hand control, with potential value for improving precision and stability on a 23-DoF hand; the limitation is that the public description is still too thin to fully assess generalization and robustness.
