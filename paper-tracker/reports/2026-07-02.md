## 条目

### 1. Human-Centric Transferable Tactile Pre-Training for Dexterous Robotic Manipulation

- 首次公开日期：2026-07-01
- 作者/机构：Chi Zhang, Penglin Cai, Ziheng Xi, Haoqi Yuan, Hao Luo, Wanpeng Zhang, Sipeng Zheng, Chaoyi Xu, Zongqing Lu
- 类型：paper
- 相关性：这篇工作把触觉感知作为灵巧操作的核心模态，并明确面向 dexterous robotic manipulation、fine-grained manipulation 和 contact-rich tasks，符合本追踪器的核心主题。
- Novelty signal：这是 7 月 1 日首发的新论文，同时公开了 H-Tac 数据集和 TTP 方法，并在模拟与真实机器人上报告结果，因此属于本轮新增且值得跟踪的进展。
- 原始链接：
  - https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01067
  - https://arxiv.org/html/2607.01067v1
- 置信度：0.95

该工作主要提出 H-Tac 与 Transferable Tactile Pre-Training (TTP) 两部分：前者是大规模人类中心触觉动作数据集，后者是在统一触觉与动作空间中进行预训练、再迁移到机器人下游任务的框架。它解决的是灵巧与接触丰富任务中“触觉数据稀缺、现有 VLA 模型过度依赖视觉、跨人机形态迁移不稳定”的问题。方法上，作者用 160 小时第一视角人类视频构建 300+ 任务、13.5 万 episode 的 H-Tac，并引入触觉专家来预测未来触觉，从而显式建模接触动力学。实验覆盖模拟和真实机器人，包含 LIBERO-long、LIBERO-plus 以及真实世界的 peeling、vase wiping、pick place chips、paper folding、plug-in 等任务。结果显示 TTP 在多项真实机器人任务上优于无触觉或弱触觉基线，且在 LIBERO-long 和零样本泛化设置上表现更好。总体来看，这是一项把触觉预训练规模化并用于人到机器人的灵巧技能迁移的工作；限制在于引入触觉后序列更长、训练成本更高，且公开信息里没有完全展开所有系统细节与失败模式。

This paper introduces two tightly coupled components: H-Tac, a large-scale human-centric tactile-action dataset, and Transferable Tactile Pre-Training (TTP), a framework that pre-trains in a unified tactile/action space and then transfers to downstream robot tasks. It addresses a clear problem in dexterous, contact-rich manipulation: tactile data are scarce, existing VLA models tend to overfit to vision, and human-to-robot transfer remains brittle when tactile representations do not match across embodiments. The method uses 160 hours of egocentric human video, more than 300 tasks, and 135k episodes, and adds a tactile expert to predict future tactile signals so the model can explicitly capture contact dynamics. Experiments span simulation and real robots, including LIBERO-long, LIBERO-plus, and real-world tasks such as peeling, vase wiping, pick-and-place chips, paper folding, and plug-in. The reported results show stronger performance than tactile-free or weaker tactile baselines, with particularly good gains on long-horizon and zero-shot generalization settings. Overall, the work is a credible step toward scalable tactile pre-training for dexterous skill transfer; the main limitation visible from the public material is that the added tactile modality increases sequence length and training cost, and the public summary does not fully expose all failure cases or implementation details.
