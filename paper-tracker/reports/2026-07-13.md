## 1. TactiDex: A Real-World Tactile-Guided Benchmark for Human-Like Dexterous Manipulation

- 首次公开：2026-07-10
- 作者/机构：Suting Ni, Hanbing Zhang, Zhenyu Wei, Guo Chen, Chixuan Zhang, Ye Shi, Jingya Wang；ShanghaiTech University, InstAdapt
- 类型：benchmark
- 相关性：这项工作直接把全手触觉、手-物交互和灵巧操作评测放在中心位置，不是把触觉当作附属传感器，而是作为人类式灵巧操作的主要建模对象。
- 新颖性信号：这是 2026-07-10 首次公开的新 arXiv 条目，同时带有项目页；它新增了真实世界触觉引导基准 `TactiDex`、`TactiSkill` 迁移框架，以及单手/双手任务的系统实验。
- 链接：arXiv, 项目页, PDF/HTML。

**analysis_zh**：该工作主要提出 TactiDex 这一真实世界触觉引导基准，并进一步给出 TactiSkill 触觉驱动迁移框架，目标是把灵巧操作从“只模仿运动轨迹”推进到“具备接触层面的类人性”。它解决的是现有人到机器人灵巧迁移过度依赖运动学轨迹、缺少物理约束与真实接触监督的问题。方法上，作者构建了对齐全手触觉信号、细粒度运动学和物体状态的多模态数据集，并用三部分触觉奖励把 guidance、human-like alignment 和 contact constraints 统一起来，训练残差跟踪策略并在真实硬件上零样本部署。实验覆盖单手和双手任务，公开材料表明该方法在 manipulation success 和 physical realism 上都优于对照方案。总体来看，这是一项把触觉从辅助信息提升为结构化监督的工作，意义在于为灵巧、类人的手-物交互建立了更真实的评测与迁移管线；限制在于当前公开信息尚未完整展开所有基线、失败案例与跨硬件泛化边界。

**analysis_en**: This paper’s main contribution is `TactiDex`, a real-world tactile-guided benchmark, together with `TactiSkill`, a tactile-driven transfer framework intended to push dexterous manipulation beyond kinematic mimicry toward contact-level human likeness. It addresses a concrete problem in human-to-robot dexterous transfer: existing pipelines mostly rely on kinematic trajectories, which can imitate motion while missing physically grounded interaction. Methodologically, the authors build a multimodal dataset that aligns whole-hand tactile signals with multi-granularity kinematic and object states, then train a residual tracking policy with a three-part tactile reward that combines guidance, human-like alignment, and contact constraints into one objective. The experiments cover both single-hand and bimanual tasks, and the public material states that TactiSkill outperforms comparison methods in manipulation success and physical realism. Overall, the work is a strong step toward tactile-aware dexterous manipulation because it makes touch a structured supervisory signal rather than a secondary input. The main limitation from the public sources is that the full set of baselines, failure cases, and cross-hardware generalization boundaries are not yet fully documented.
