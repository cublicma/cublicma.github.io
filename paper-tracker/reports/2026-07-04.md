- analysis_zh：该工作主要提出 VT-WAM，一个视觉-触觉世界动作模型，目标是在接触丰富操作中同时预测未来视觉、触觉变形与动作。它解决的是现有视觉-触觉策略常把触觉直接喂给动作头、却很少显式建模接触阶段触觉动态的问题，因此在滑移、压力和局部变形等细粒度线索上更容易失真。方法上，作者采用统一的 flow matching 框架，并用非对称 Mixture-of-Transformers 注意力连接首帧视觉锚点与时序触觉动态，再用接触门控的 AVTAG 促使动作查询在接触阶段依赖触觉证据。实验覆盖 6 个真实世界接触丰富操作任务，结果显示平均成功率达到 71.67%，优于 Fast-WAM 和 OmniVTLA；消融实验也支持建模触觉变形与接触阶段触觉注意力的重要性。总体上，这是一项把触觉从辅助输入提升为动态建模对象的工作，适合灵巧操作场景；限制在于公开摘要尚未充分披露硬件细节、任务构成和失败模式的完整分析。
- analysis_en: This paper introduces VT-WAM, a Visual-Tactile World Action Model for contact-rich manipulation, with the main contribution being joint prediction of future visual states, tactile deformation, and actions in a single model. It addresses a practical problem in dexterous manipulation: existing visual-tactile policies often pass tactile inputs directly to the action head, but they do not explicitly model tactile dynamics during contact, where slip, pressure, and local deformation matter most. Methodologically, the authors use a unified flow-matching framework, asymmetric Mixture-of-Transformers attention to connect a first-frame visual anchor with temporal tactile dynamics, and a contact-gated AVTAG mechanism to bias action queries toward tactile evidence during contact phases. The experiments cover six real-world contact-rich manipulation tasks, and the reported average success rate is 71.67%, outperforming Fast-WAM and OmniVTLA; ablations further support the value of modeling tactile deformation and guiding contact-phase tactile attention. Overall, the work makes tactile dynamics a first-class modeling target for dexterous manipulation. The main limitation from the public material is that hardware details, task composition, and failure analysis are not yet fully disclosed.
