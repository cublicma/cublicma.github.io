## 条目

### 1. DexVerse: A Modular Benchmark for Multi-Task, Multi-Embodiment Dexterous Manipulation

- 首次公开：2026-07-10
- 作者/组织：Yunchao Yao, Zhuxiu Xu, Tianqi Zhang, Zixian Liu, Sikai Li, Zhenyu Wei, Feng Chen, Dihong Huang, Kechang Wan, Chenyang Ma, Shuqi Zhao, Shenghua Gao, Masayoshi Tomizuka, Yi Ma, Mingyu Ding
- 类型：benchmark
- 相关性：它把灵巧操作评测提升到多任务、多具身、可控视觉变化的统一基准层面，灵巧机器人手是核心主题，而不是附属场景。
- 新颖性：2026-07-10 首次公开；同时给出项目页、VR 遥操作接口和 3,180 条演示数据，属于明显的新基准发布。
- 链接：
  - https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08751
  - https://arxiv.org/html/2607.08751
  - https://ycyao216.github.io/DexVerse.site

DexVerse 的主要工作是构建一个面向通用灵巧操作策略的模块化大规模基准，解决现有评测在任务多样性、具身覆盖和视觉扰动控制上的不足。方法上，它组织了 100 个任务，覆盖抓取与搬运、关节物体交互、工具使用、双手协调、非预抓取、接触丰富行为、多目标与长时程阶段式任务，并支持 3 种机械臂和 6 种灵巧手，还提供可配置的纹理、背景、光照和相机变化，以及 VR 遥操作接口与 3,180 条带 proprioception、RGB、depth、point-cloud 和 state 的演示。实验上，作者在 19 个任务上评测 Diffusion Policy、DP3、OpenVLA 和 π0.5，结果显示任务泛化与视觉鲁棒性仍然很难。总结来看，DexVerse 是一个很强的通用灵巧操作测试床；限制在于它主要是基准与评测平台，公开结果也表明现有方法离真正的跨任务、跨具身泛化还有明显差距。

DexVerse’s main contribution is a modular large-scale benchmark for general-purpose dexterous manipulation, addressing the limitations of existing evaluations in task diversity, embodiment coverage, and controllable visual variation. The method organizes 100 tasks spanning grasping and relocation, articulated-object interaction, tool use, bimanual coordination, non-prehensile control, contact-rich behaviors, multi-goal execution, and long-horizon staged tasks, while supporting 3 robot arms and 6 dexterous hands. It also provides configurable textures, backgrounds, lighting, and camera viewpoints, plus a VR teleoperation interface and 3,180 demonstrations with synchronized proprioception, RGB, depth, point-cloud, and state observations. In experiments, the authors benchmark Diffusion Policy, DP3, OpenVLA, and π0.5 across 19 tasks, and the results show that task generalization and visuomotor robustness remain difficult. Overall, DexVerse is a strong testbed for dexterous manipulation research; the main limitation is that it is primarily a benchmark platform, and the public results also suggest existing methods are still far from robust cross-task and cross-embodiment generalization.

### 2. AnyDexRT: Calibration-Free Dexterous Hand Retargeting with Few-Shot Human Guidance

- 首次公开：2026-07-09
- 作者/组织：Chenxi Wang, Ying Feng, Hongjie Fang, Shangning Xia, Lixin Yang, Chuan Wen, Cewu Lu
- 类型：paper
- 相关性：它直接服务于灵巧手遥操作与示教数据采集，解决不同灵巧手之间的重定向问题，属于本主题的核心基础方法。
- 新颖性：2026-07-09 首次公开，并附带项目页；相较常见手工调参或强依赖标定的方案，它强调无标定与 few-shot 人类引导。
- 链接：
  - https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08341
  - https://arxiv.org/html/2607.08341
  - https://chenxi-wang.github.io/projects/anydexrt

AnyDexRT 的主要工作是为人类到灵巧手的运动重定向提供一种无标定方案，以缓解现有遥操作系统对手工目标函数、精确标定和全局形状匹配的依赖。方法上，它先用自监督的 fingertip correspondence 学习建立人手与机器人手在任务相关区域的对应，再用 few-shot human guidance 锚定映射，最后通过 contact classifier 细化 pinch 类姿态，从而减少冗余动作空间带来的歧义。实验上，作者在多种灵巧手和真实遥操作任务上验证，展示了更好的重定向质量、更少的手动调参以及更直观高效的控制。结果表明该方法适合跨手型复用，并能提升示教与操控体验。限制在于公开信息主要来自摘要和项目页，尚不足以完整判断其在极端姿态、复杂接触和更大规模真实部署中的稳健性与泛化边界。

AnyDexRT’s main contribution is a calibration-free retargeting method for mapping human motions to dexterous robot hands, addressing the reliance of existing teleoperation systems on hand-crafted objectives, precise calibration, and global shape matching. The method first learns fingertip correspondence in a self-supervised way to align human and robot hands in task-relevant regions, then uses few-shot human guidance to anchor the mapping, and finally refines pinch-like poses with a contact classifier to reduce ambiguity in redundant action spaces. In experiments across diverse dexterous hands and real-world teleoperation tasks, the authors report better retargeting quality, less manual tuning, and more intuitive and efficient control than prior methods. The results suggest the approach is practical for cross-hand reuse and for improving demonstration collection. The main limitation is that the public information is still mostly the abstract and project page, so the robustness boundaries in extreme poses, complex contacts, and larger-scale deployments remain insufficiently documented.
