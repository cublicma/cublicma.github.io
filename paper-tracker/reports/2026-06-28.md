这项工作的主要工作是提出 Play2Perfect，一种先通过任务无关的“玩耍式”强化学习预训练、再针对精密装配精调的框架。它解决了多指灵巧手在精密装配中面临的接触丰富、稀疏奖励和直接探索困难的问题。方法上，作者让策略在多样物体与目标上学习可复用的操作先验，包括抓取、手内重定向和位姿到达，再将该先验迁移到装配阶段，并系统分析物体多样性、训练目标、轨迹多样性与目标精度等设计因素。实验显示，该先验比从零开始的 RL 训练样本效率高 33 倍，并实现零样本仿真到真实转移，在仅 0.5 mm 接触间隙的紧配合插入上达到 60% 成功率，在长时程多部件装配和拧螺丝任务上也超过 50% 成功率。总体来看，这项工作证明了“先学会玩”对精密灵巧装配很有价值，但公开信息里对硬件细节、失败模式和更广泛任务泛化的限制披露仍不充分。

The main contribution is Play2Perfect, a reinforcement-learning framework that first pretrains through task-agnostic play and then finetunes for precise assembly. It addresses a core problem in multi-finger dexterous manipulation: contact-rich assembly is hard to learn because data collection for imitation is difficult and direct RL exploration is sparse-reward and unstable. Methodologically, the authors train a reusable manipulation prior on diverse objects and goals, targeting grasping, in-hand reorientation, and pose reaching, then transfer that prior to assembly; they also study key design choices such as object diversity, training objective, trajectory diversity, and goal precision. Experiments show the prior is 33x more sample-efficient than training RL from scratch, and the system achieves zero-shot sim-to-real transfer, with 60% success on tight insertions at only 0.5 mm contact clearance and over 50% success on long-horizon multi-part assembly and screwing. Overall, the paper makes a strong case that playful pretraining is useful for precise dexterous assembly, but the public information leaves some limitations under-specified, especially around hardware details, failure modes, and broader generalization.
