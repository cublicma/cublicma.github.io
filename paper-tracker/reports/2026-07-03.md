## 条目

### 1. Imagining the Sense of Touch: Touch-Informed Manipulation via Imagined Tactile Representations

**类型**：paper  
**首发日期**：2026-07-02  
**作者/机构**：Xiao Ma, Yilun Hao, Nitin J. Sanket, Tatsuya Ikeda, C. Karen Liu, Shaoxiong Wang  
**相关性**：这项工作把触觉表征作为灵巧接触操作的核心建模对象，直接面向抓取、重定向、推、抓放等接触丰富任务，属于灵巧操作方向的实质性进展。  
**Novelty**：这是 7 月 2 日新公开的 arXiv 论文，提出了 Imagined Tactile Representation (ITR) 与触觉想象框架，并报告了基准与实机结果，因此是本轮新增条目。  
**链接**：https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01684, https://tacimag.github.io/  

**analysis_zh**：该工作主要提出 Imagined Tactile Representation（ITR）与 Touch-Informed Manipulation 框架，目标是在没有真实触觉传感器或触觉硬件受限的情况下，让机器人仍能利用“想象出的触觉”进行灵巧接触操作。它解决的是触觉信号难采集、跨硬件迁移困难以及接触丰富任务中仅靠视觉与本体感觉不稳定的问题。方法上，作者从人类触觉动作数据中学习触觉表征，并将其用于操作策略的预测与控制，同时构建了面向接触操作的评测设置。实验涵盖模拟与真实机器人任务，涉及抓取、重定向、推拨和抓放等典型灵巧操作。结果表明，引入 ITR 后，策略在接触丰富任务中更稳健，能提升操作成功率与泛化能力。总体来看，这是一项把触觉“补全”为可迁移表征的工作，意义在于降低灵巧操作对昂贵触觉硬件的依赖；限制在于公开信息里尚未完全展开全部训练细节、失败案例与对不同机器人平台的系统消融。  
**analysis_en**：This paper introduces Imagined Tactile Representation (ITR) and a touch-informed manipulation framework aimed at enabling dexterous contact-rich behavior even when real tactile hardware is absent or hard to scale. The main contribution is to treat touch as a learnable representation rather than a required sensor stream, which addresses a practical problem in dexterous manipulation: tactile data are expensive, hard to transfer across embodiments, and visual/proprioceptive signals alone are often brittle at contact. Methodologically, the authors learn tactile representations from human touch-action data and use them to predict and guide manipulation policies, then evaluate the approach in contact-centric settings. The experiments cover both simulation and real-robot tasks, including grasping, reorientation, pushing, and pick-and-place style interactions. The reported results indicate that adding ITR improves robustness and success on contact-rich tasks and helps generalization. Overall, the work is a meaningful step toward scalable tactile reasoning for dexterous manipulation; the main limitation from the public material is that not all training details, failure modes, and cross-platform ablations are fully exposed yet.
